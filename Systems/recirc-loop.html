Hot Water Recirculating Loop

Systems > Water > Hot Water Recirculating Loop

A recirculating hot water loop is a way to design the hot water plumbing to minimize energy and water waste. It consists of a water pump located at the hot water heater connected to a pipe that runs throughout the house near all hot water taps. The pump is activated by a motion detector in each bathroom and the laundry room, and a push-button in the kitchen.

When someone enters a bathroom, the motion detector turns on the pump, which then pumps hot water into the recirculation loop pipe, and the cold contents of the pipe come back into the water heater to be reheated. After a few seconds, the cooled-off water in the pipe has been replaced with hot water from the tank. Then, when the person in the bathroom turns on the hot water tap, it is only necessary to run out the water from the short pipe that branches off the recirculation loop to the tap before the water is hot.

The recirculation pump is set to turn off a short time after it starts, calculated to replace the water in the recirculation loop, and not continue to run pointlessly after that.

Without the recirculation loop, the person would run the hot water until it became hot, letting the water in pipes all the way back to the water heater just run down the drain, wasted.

Depending on the design of the plumbing system and the usage patterns of the building occupants, a recirculating design can also save significant amounts of energy involved in heating water that then just cools off in pipes.
